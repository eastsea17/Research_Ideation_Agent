#1 Forecasting Technological Paradigm Shifts using Dynamic Temporal Graph Neural Networks on Global Patent Data

Score: 12/15
Background
Current patent network analysis is predominantly static and descriptive, focusing on retrospective snapshots of citation networks within a single jurisdiction. This fails to capture the dynamic, cross-border evolution of technology necessary to anticipate major shifts.
Necessity
Static networks cannot model the temporal dependencies and international knowledge flows that precede disruptive innovations. Existing methods lack the predictive power to answer critical 'what-if' questions for R&D strategy and science policy.

  • 5. Case Studies: Applying the Model to AI, Quantum Computing, and Synthetic Biology
  • 6. Policy and Strategic Implications
Expected Effects
Enable prediction of technology convergence points 3-5 years in advance with >70% accuracy, providing a quantitative tool for governments and corporations to de-risk R&D investments and anticipate new markets.

#2 The Geopolitics of Innovation: Mapping and Quantifying Knowledge Flow Barriers and Strategic Competition in Patent Networks

Score: 12/15
Background
Patent analysis typically treats the global innovation system as a single, homogeneous network. This ignores the profound impact of geopolitical borders, export controls, and national strategic competition on the flow of knowledge.
Necessity
Current O-I index and similar analyses fail to account for non-technical barriers to knowledge diffusion. This results in a fundamentally inaccurate model of how technology actually evolves in a world of increasing techno-nationalism.
Table of Contents
  • 1. Modeling Geopolitical Boundaries in Knowledge Networks
  • 2. Data Integration: Linking Patent Data with Policy Data (e.g., Entity Lists, Export Controls)
  • 3. Network Metrics for Strategic Dependence and Technological Sovereignty
  • 4. Simulating the Impact of Policy Interventions (e.g., Decoupling) on Innovation Rates
  • 5. Case Studies: Semiconductor, AI, and Pharmaceutical Industries
  • 6. A New Framework for Science and Technology Diplomacy
Expected Effects
Quantify the 'friction' imposed by geopolitics on global innovation, providing policymakers with a simulation tool to assess the long-term costs and benefits of technological decoupling and identify critical dependencies for national security.
Related Papers

Evaluation

Impact

Reasoning: The proposal introduces a novel angle by explicitly integrating geopolitical borders, export controls, and strategic competition into patent network analysis, which is largely absent from current O‑I and related studies. While the conceptual framework is original, the practical implementation will require assembling heterogeneous datasets (patent metadata, geopolitical risk indices, trade‑control lists) and developing sophisticated network‑friction metrics, making it moderately challenging but still realistic with interdisciplinary collaboration. The potential impact is high: a quantitative model of geopolitical friction in innovation could inform national security decisions, guide technology‑decoupling strategies, and identify critical dependencies, offering significant value to policymakers and academia alike.

#3 Generative Invention: A Foundation Language Model for Semantic Invention Graphs and Novel Concept Synthesis

Score: 11/15
Background
While 'intelligent' patent analysis automates keyword extraction, it misses the deep semantic meaning of inventions. The relationships between patents are superficial, based on citations or co-occurrence, not on the functional or conceptual principles they embody.
Necessity
Keyword-based networks are incapable of representing the underlying principles of an invention, making them useless for generative tasks. There is no existing AI system that can read the entirety of patent knowledge and propose novel, technically plausible inventions.
Table of Contents
  • 1. From Keywords to Concepts: The Case for Semantic Invention Graphs
  • 2. Pre-training and Fine-tuning an LLM on a Global Patent Corpus
  • 3. Constructing the Graph: Nodes as Functional Units, Edges as Semantic Relationships
  • 4. The Generative Framework: Using Graph-to-Text Models for Novel Claim Drafting
  • 5. Evaluating Novelty and Usefulness: Benchmarking Against Expert Inventors
  • 6. Ethical Considerations and the Future of AI-Assisted Invention
Expected Effects
Develop a model that can generate novel, patent-like claims by combining concepts from disparate technology domains, potentially reducing the ideation phase of R&D by an order of magnitude and creating a measurable benchmark for machine creativity.
